Our Mission

The Institute for Computational Sociology bridges the formal rigor of computational science with the interpretive depth of social inquiry. We believe that the grand challenges of our time — inequality, polarization, institutional decay, and the social consequences of technology — require new methods and new alliances across disciplines.

We are committed to open science, reproducible research, and to ensuring that the insights we generate reach policymakers, practitioners, and the public — not only academic journals.

—

Research Pillars

Digital Inequality

How does access to and use of digital platforms reproduce or challenge existing social stratification?

Algorithmic Governance

Studying how automated decision systems shape opportunities, constrain choices, and redistribute power.

Computational Methods

Developing NLP, network science, and simulation methods adapted for sociological theory.

Policy & Society

Translating computational findings into evidence for social policy at national and international scales.
